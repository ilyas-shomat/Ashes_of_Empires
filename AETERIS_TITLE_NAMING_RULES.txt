AETERIS TITLE-NAMING RULES
==========================

Purpose
-------
These rules define a coherent naming language for Aeteris titles and settlements.
The desired sound lies between the elevated place-names of Numenor, Gondor, and
Arnor and historical Turkic-Iranian phonology. It must evoke both sources without
copying a Tolkien name or using a real modern Turkish or Iranian personal/place
name unchanged.


1. WHAT THE EXISTING AETERIS NAMES ALREADY DO
----------------------------------------------

The names in map_data/definition.csv use two related registers:

  A. Courtly / old imperial:
     Annoras, Arzanor, Elanar, Belaras, Rovandar, Talgaron, Valoras, Rethanor,
     Voramir, Calduran, Malethor, Eranzir, Torvazan, Avelkar.

     These favor three syllables, liquid consonants, and endings such as -anor,
     -aras, -iran, -amir, -uran, -ethor, -zir, and -azan.

  B. Frontier / successor-realm:
     Tir-Valen, Dar-Moren, Tor-Veyr, Val-Adar, Dar-Kalen, Val-Soren, Ar-Veyr,
     Tir-Sarun, Tor-Kharan, Kar-Noral.

     These combine a one-syllable geographic element with a two-syllable root.
     Short unprefixed forms such as Varen, Mareth, Ordan, Sareth, Velan, and
     Selkar belong to the same layer.

Future names should preserve both registers. Old capitals, kingdoms, and ancient
duchies normally use the courtly register. Marches, forts, valleys, and younger
counties normally use the compound frontier register.


2. SOUND INVENTORY
------------------

Preferred consonants:
  b, d, f, g, h, k, kh, l, m, n, r, s, sh, t, v, y, z

Use sparingly:
  c (always a hard k-like sound), th, ph

Avoid:
  j, ch, soft g, w, x, q, and dense consonant clusters

Preferred vowels:
  a, e, i, o, u

Common vowel movement:
  a-a, a-e, a-i, e-a, e-i, o-a, o-e, u-a

Most names should alternate consonants and vowels. Permit at most one marked
cluster per name: kh, sh, th, nd, ld, rn, rz, or rv. This keeps the Iranian-
Turkic color without losing the smooth Numenorean cadence.

Stress should usually fall on the first or penultimate syllable:
  VA-lo-ras, re-THA-nor, TOR-va-zan, SE-lkar.


3. BUILDING ROOTS
-----------------

Construct a root from two invented sound units. Do not begin with a complete real
name and merely change its spelling.

Opening units:
  Ael-, Arv-, Azel-, Bel-, Cal-, Darv-, Elv-, Er-, Far-, Ilar-, Kal-, Khar-,
  Mal-, Marv-, Nar-, Nem-, Orv-, Reth-, Rov-, Sarv-, Sel-, Talv-, Tir-, Torv-,
  Val-, Vel-, Vor-, Zarv-

Middle units:
  -ad-, -al-, -an-, -ar-, -az-, -el-, -en-, -er-, -esh-, -eth-, -ir-, -or-,
  -un-, -ur-, -uz-

Root endings:
  -an, -ar, -as, -ath, -az, -en, -er, -eth, -ir, -is, -on, -or, -un, -ur

Examples of constructed roots:
  Arvel, Belzuran, Calreth, Elvar, Ilarun, Kharven, Marveth, Nemraz, Orvel,
  Rethun, Sarvel, Talvaz, Velrath, Vorzen, Zarvun.

Reject a root if it is already a familiar modern Turkish/Iranian name, surname,
city, province, ethnonym, dynasty, or common title.


4. GEOGRAPHIC PREFIXES
----------------------

These are Aeteric naming particles, not literal borrowings. Use them consistently:

  Ar-    royal, high, first, or founded by the crown
  Dar-   enclosed settlement, citadel, or seat of administration
  Kar-   rocky stronghold or mountain ward
  Tir-   watch, tower, beacon, or guarded height
  Tor-   hill, ridge, or fortified rise
  Val-   vale, river basin, fertile lowland, or sheltered country

Write the particle and root with a hyphen for transparent younger names:
  Tir-Orvel, Dar-Calreth, Tor-Sarvel, Val-Ilarun.

Fuse them in ancient names when centuries of use would have obscured the compound:
  Ar + vel + anor -> Arvelanor
  Tor + vaz + an  -> Torvazan
  Val + or + as   -> Valoras

Do not attach a prefix to a root beginning with the same sound. Avoid forms such
as Tor-Torven, Val-Valen, or Dar-Darun.


5. RANK AND REGISTER
--------------------

Empire / kingdom:
  Use one ancient, fused name of three or four syllables. Favor -anor, -aras,
  -eron, -oria, or -uran, but never copy Arnor, Gondor, Numenor, or their exact
  compounds.
  Patterns: ROOT + imperial ending; fused PARTICLE + ROOT + ending.
  Examples: Arvelanor, Belzuran, Calveras, Ilareron, Zarvethor.

Duchy / major principality:
  Use a strong three-syllable name or a prestigious geographic compound.
  Patterns: ROOT + -ar/-as/-on/-or; Tir-/Dar-/Val- + ROOT.
  Examples: Marvethor, Nemrazan, Rovelar, Tir-Orvel, Val-Calreth.

County:
  Use two or three syllables. Favor a plain root, a softened ending, or a fused
  old compound.
  Examples: Arvel, Belzaran, Elvaren, Rethun, Selvar, Talveth.

Barony / settlement:
  Use a short root or a transparent geographic compound. Neighboring baronies
  may share one root family, but must not repeat the county name mechanically.
  Examples: Velrath, Orven, Dar-Nemir, Kar-Zarvun, Tor-Beles, Tir-Marven.


6. IRANIAN-TURKIC COLOR WITHOUT DIRECT BORROWING
------------------------------------------------

Draw inspiration from sound features, not from whole words or living names:

  - Use kh, sh, z, v, and final -r/-n/-d in moderation.
  - Use open a vowels beside narrower e/i vowels.
  - Allow endings reminiscent of -dar, -zar, -mir, -van, or -stan only after
    changing both their form and their implied meaning: e.g. -daran, -zarel,
    -miran, -var, -astan. Never use -stan as the default country suffix.
  - Balance a hard Iranian-Turkic element with a liquid or elevated element:
    Khar + el + on = Kharelon; Zarv + eth + or = Zarvethor.
  - Avoid strings that are merely two recognizable real words joined together.

Names to avoid reusing unchanged include obvious real forms already present or
suggested by the draft palette: Yashar/Yasar, Sarim, Shahmir, Temur, Mirza,
Kadesh, Aman, Aram, Salar, Tairan, and modern country/city/region names. Existing
map names can remain as legacy material, but new titles should not extend this
direct-borrowing habit.


7. TOLKIEN-DISTANCE RULE
------------------------

A new name must pass all of these tests:

  1. It is not an attested Tolkien name with one letter changed.
  2. It does not use a famous stem plus a famous ending (for example Gond- + -or).
  3. It does not reproduce Quenya/Sindarin grammar unless that grammar is part of
     an explicitly defined Aeteric borrowing.
  4. It shares atmosphere and cadence, not exact vocabulary.

Therefore prefer Arvelanor over a form like Arnumenor, and Calveras over a form
like Gondaras.


8. DUPLICATION AND QUALITY CHECK
--------------------------------

Before accepting a name:

  1. Search the entire mod case-insensitively for the complete name.
  2. Search for its root without the prefix and ending.
  3. Check that no sibling title begins and ends with the same units.
  4. Check it against a list of modern Turkish and Iranian given names, surnames,
     and major place-names.
  5. Say it aloud three times. Reject it if pronunciation is unclear.
  6. Ensure it is visually distinct from its liege and capital.
  7. Use lowercase ASCII for CK3 title keys even when the display name contains a
     hyphen: display "Tir-Orvel" -> key d_tir_orvel.

Within one duchy, use no more than:
  - one name ending in -anor;
  - one name ending in -mir/-miran;
  - two names beginning with the same letter;
  - two hyphenated particle names.


9. QUICK GENERATION FORMULAS
----------------------------

Ancient realm:
  opening + middle + imperial ending
  Arv + el + anor = Arvelanor

Old noble province:
  opening + middle + rank ending
  Zarv + eth + or = Zarvethor

Frontier duchy:
  geographic particle + invented root
  Tir + Orvel = Tir-Orvel

Fortified county:
  geographic particle + two-syllable root
  Dar + Calreth = Dar-Calreth

Small settlement:
  opening + short ending
  Nem + ir = Nemir


10. SAMPLE NEW TITLE SET
------------------------

The following illustrate the rules and are not automatically assigned:

  Kingdom:   k_arvelanor     "Arvelanor"
  Duchy:     d_zarvethor     "Zarvethor"
  Duchy:     d_tir_orvel     "Tir-Orvel"
  County:    c_calreth       "Calreth"
  County:    c_belzaran      "Belzaran"
  County:    c_rethun        "Rethun"
  Barony:    b_dar_nemir     "Dar-Nemir"
  Barony:    b_tor_sarvel    "Tor-Sarvel"
  Barony:    b_kar_zarvun    "Kar-Zarvun"
  Barony:    b_velrath       "Velrath"

The central principle: invent the root first, apply Aeteric geography second,
and test against both real-world and Tolkien names last.
